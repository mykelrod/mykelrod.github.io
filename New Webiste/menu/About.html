History


AC POWER Plant Maintenance Services or ACPPMS was established on October 2018, it was developed and stablished base on individual knowledge and expertise of four individual person where their experience since from the start is on fabrication, commissioning and maintenance of power plants. Two of these partners came from National Power Corporation (NPC) before it had been privatized. And the other two partners are coming from private power plant company. Their 30 years’ experience in maintenance, installation, repair, fabrication and commissioning of Rotating and Non-Rotating parts of power plant will be their best asset in power plant maintenance and overhauling. They have a long history of providing client services, sustainable engineering practices and forward-thinking solutions. Before ACPPMS was stablished, we engage and partnered to different stablished group of contractors and serve to their power plant clients. In so doing, our key personnel decided to put up our own company that’s how AC POWER (ACPPMS) was born.

ACPPMS have a team of highly-skilled and well-experienced personnel in maintenance and repair, project management services, fabrication and related engineering works. ACPPMS offers the best-in-class services in the industry for mechanical engineering services for a broad range of clients.











































Occupational Safety and Health (OSH) Program


AC POWER PLANT MAINTENANCE SERVICES do here by commit to comply with the requirements of RA 11058 and DOLE Department Order 198-18 (Its Implementing Rules and Regulations) and the applicable provisions of the Occupational Safety and Health Standards (OSHS).

We acknowledge the company’s obligation and responsibilities to provide appropriate funds for implementing this OSH program including orientation and training of its employees on OSH, provision and dissemination of IEC materials on safety and health, provision of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) when necessary and other OSH related requirements and activities, to ensure the protection for our workers and employees against injuries, illnesses and death through safe and healthy working conditions and environment.

We commit to conduct risk assessment as required to prevent workplace accidents as well as comply with other provisions of this OSH program. That we are also fully aware of the penalties and sanctions for OSH violations as provided for in RA 11058 and its Implementing Rules and Regulations.











































Workplace Prevention of SARS-Cov-2


A new virus called the severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) was identified as the cause of a disease outbreak that began in China 2019. The disease is called coronavirus disease 2019(COVID-19).

It is on March 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) declared COVID-19 a pandemic. Treat to national security and prompting a whole government approach in addressing the outbreak, declared a State of Public Health emergency throughout the entire Philippines through Proclamation No.922.

On April 23, 2020, the IATF recommend that guidelines be issued by the different government agencies to provide policy directions for the public and stakeholders in complying with government regulations following the modification of ECQ.



Objectives


We at AC POWER Plant Maintenance Services started initiating awareness to our employees on how to safeguard and protect themselves and the society from deadly wide spreading COVID- 19 virus.

By giving awareness like the following:


1. How does COVID-19 spread?

Data has shown that it spreads from person to person among those in close contact. The virus spreads thru respiratory droplets.


2. What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

Covid-19 symptoms can be very mild to severe and include a fever, cough and shortness of breath. Some people have no symptoms. Symptoms may appear two to 14 days after exposure. People may be sick with the virus for 1 to 14 days before developing symptoms. The most common symptoms of corona virus disease (COVID-19) are fever, tiredness, and dry cough. Most people (about 80%) recover from the disease without needing special treatment. More rarely, the disease can be serious and even fatal.

Older people, and people with other medical conditions (such as asthma, diabetes, or heart disease), may be more vulnerable to becoming severely ill.

    People may experience:
  • Cough
  • Fever
  • Tiredness
  • Difficulty breathing (severe cases)

    • 3. Can COVID-19 be prevented or treated?

    A vaccine isn’t currently available to the public for the coronavirus disease (COVID-19).Treatment is directed at relieving symptoms.


      4. What can I do to avoid becoming ill?

  • Avoid large events and mass gatherings.
  • Avoid close contact (within 6 feet, or 2 meters) with anyone who is sick has symptoms.
  • Keep distance between yourself and others if COVID-19 is spreading in your community, if you have a higher risk of serious illness.
  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol.
  • Cover your mouth and nose with your elbow or a tissue when you cough sneeze. Throw away the used tissue at bin.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth
  • Clean and disinfect surface you often touch on a daily basis

    Hazardous Waste Handling and Disposal


    Personnel Training All employees working in the project exposed to hazardous substances, health hazards or safety hazards including the supervisors and management responsible for the project site shall be trained before they permitted to engage in hazardous waste operations. Periodic refresher training must be conducted. To minimize employee risk and company liability. AC POWER has established the following employee training programs; Hazard Communication and a Spill Response Program. The hazard communication program includes an introduction to Hazardous Waste Management and Labelling. Emphasis is placed on the use of Safety Data Sheet (SDS). The spill response program includes spill control, containment and decontamination procedure.
